About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Leadership, Policy, & Organization in the Peabody College of Education at Vanderbilt University. I conduct research on teacher labor market etc....that focuses on analysis of social structures that interact with the built and natural environment to promote or harm social equity, ecological and human health, and long-term viability of social-environmental systems with a methodological emphasis on consideration of spatial and temporal dependencies. I have MS and BS degrees in Environmental Engineering from Washington University in St. Louis and the University of Oklahoma, respectively. Prior to my doctoral studies at Vanderbilt University I served as the Lab Manager and Senior Scientist of the National Science Foundation funded Nano Research Facility at Washington University in St. Louis.

Research

Investigating the drivers of resilience, vulnerability, and adaptive capacity and their impacts on social-environmental systems using spatiotemporally explicit modeling and behavior driver simulation methodologies within an interdisciplinary framing.

Teaching

Preparing students for roles as future scientists, engineers, and decision-makers through interdisciplinary, problem-based and investigative learning.
