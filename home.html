About Me

I am a doctoral candidate and research assistant in the Department of Leadership, Policy, and Organizations at Vanderbilt University. I taught middle and high school math for seven years in St. Louis, Missouri before resuming my graduate studies at Peabody. I hold a dual-degree in Math and Physics from the University of Oklahoma and a Master of Arts in Teaching from Washington University in St. Louis.

Research

(1) Investigating teacher leadership and school improvement in a research-practice partnership; (2) teacher policies and teacher labor markets; and (3) distribution of financial aid and its effects on postsecondary persistence and degree attainment.

Teaching

Helping students build content mastery with practical applications.

Diversity

Dedicated to the study and promotion of diversity and equity in education.
